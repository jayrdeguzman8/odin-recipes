Pork Adobo

Pork Adobo is a classic Filipino dish where tender pork is simmered in a flavorful mix of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices. It’s known for its savory, tangy, and slightly sweet taste, often served with steamed rice.

This dish is perfect for any meal, especially with its rich, savory flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork (usually shoulder or belly, cut into chunks)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 onion, sliced (optional)
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying (optional)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Pork: Combine the pork, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Let it marinate for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Cook the Pork: In a pot, add the marinated pork with the marinade, vinegar, and water. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 40-45 minutes until the pork is tender and the sauce thickens.
  3. Fry (optional): For a crispy texture, you can fry the pork pieces in oil after they’ve simmered.
  4. Season: Add sugar if you like a hint of sweetness. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve: Serve the Pork Adobo with steamed rice and enjoy!
